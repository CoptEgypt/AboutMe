My Story

In 2010, my family began seriously considering the idea of moving from Egypt to the United States. While my family was living comfortably in Egypt, religious persecutions and terrorist attacks were increasing which made it difficult for me to continue my education peacefully, even though I was 10 years old and I was living a happy childhood. Terrorist attacks led my family to decide to immigrate to the United States. Suddenly my childhood was replaced by responsibilities such as caring for my two-year-old sister and translating from English to Arabic for my parents. It made me jealous to see my friends in Egypt living their daily lives peacefully while I am here living a different life. I still couldnâ€™t believe that the country where I was born and raised, the country I love, had become a breeding ground for extreme prejudice. I started thinking about the ways that I could help prevent more violence, and when I did my research I realized that terrorists get their information by hacking the government's database. An idea began to take shape in my mind. A career in Computer Science would provide me with a concrete way to combat and prevent terrorist organizations from spreading. Armed with the knowledge and education I would receive. I could focus my work on discovering and identifying terrorist and illegal organizations.

Over the past few years, I have pursued many opportunities related to computer science. My first collaborative project was to help create a website. I had the chance to work with others and learn different coding languages such as HTML coding, Java coding, the asp.net framework, and HTTPS. Using what I learned, I was able to create a website with event schedules, announcements, Calls to Action, media resources, and video and audio content. I later started working at Fred Meyer in Mill Creek, WA in 2018 as a payroll coordinator. The company realized how interested I was in working with technology, so I was tasked by management to help improve our computer systems. I was able to identify and implement usability and connectivity updates, such as implementing a code for payroll, which led to significant improvements to the store's systems.

Being admitted to the University of Washington Bothell to the Computer Science and Software Engineering program is important to me because I value the multicultural community that I experienced with the shared campus with Cascadia. Attending U.W Bothell would bring me joy and opportunities to collaborate and develop my skills and expertise to develop solutions to help communities, whether in the U.S or Egypt.
